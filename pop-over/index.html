      Behaviour spec

      Width — width: fit-content hugs the longest visible label, capped at max-width: 40rem. Beyond that, labels truncate with ellipsis; hover shows a tooltip only when the label is actually truncated.

      Section order — Quick links (user-defined, labelled) come first and scroll in the middle region. Promotional links (system, no label) are pinned at the bottom — the block is always visible, and the list scrolls internally when height is tight.

      Scroll strategy (toggle)

      Smart scroll (default) — promo pinned at the bottom; prefer one section scrollbar when possible, dual scroll when both sections overflow.

      Single scroll — one scrollbar on the links area; quick links and promo scroll together. Promo is not always visible — scroll to reach it.

      Height strategy (toggle)

      Constrain (default) — pop-over max height = viewport − header − margin. Links scroll inside the menu; all items stay reachable.

      Overflow viewport — no 28rem cap; pop-over fills the viewport slot. When content exceeds that slot, quick links scroll internally and promo stays pinned at the bottom (same split logic as constrain). Red outline when content would extend past the viewport.

      Height-responsive layout (constrain mode)

      Prefer one scrollbar when only one section overflows. When both overflow, use two independent scroll panes — the only way to keep every item reachable while promo stays pinned.

      • Quick-only — all promo visible; quick links scroll
      • Promo-only — all quick links visible; promo scrolls
      • Both — each section scrolls within its allocation (min. 2 quick rows + 1 promo row)
      • None — everything fits

      Zone Minimum Why

      Allocation order

      1. Everything fits → hug content, no scroll
      2. Promo fits in budget → show all promo; quick links get the rest (≥ minimum)
      3. Quick links fit in budget → show all quick; promo gets the rest (≥ minimum)
      4. Neither fits → reserve both minimums, split any extra 60% quick / 40% promo
      5. Budget below combined minimums → 60 / 40 split; both scroll; nothing clipped

      Quick links win on priority (user content); promo wins on visibility (always pinned, never zero).

      Priority when space is tight

      1. Search bar — always visible
      2. Reserve min-quick and min-promo when both sections exist
      3. Give surplus space to quick links first, then promo
      4. Enable scroll per section only when its content exceeds its allocation
